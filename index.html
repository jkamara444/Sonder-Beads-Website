History of Waistbeads

Waistbeads originated in the vibrant cultural milieu of the Igbo people. In the Igbo culture, waistbeads symbolized rites of passage, femininity, and spirituality. Crafted with precision and adorned with a spectrum of colors and materials, waistbeads became an integral part of traditional Igbo attire. The meticulous craftsmanship reflected the cultural values of the Igbo people, encapsulating their deep connection to spirituality and the celebration of womanhood. Read more here!
